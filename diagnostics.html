  • What is an ECG/EKG Test?

    An ECG, or EKG (electrocardiogram), is a diagnostic test that records the heart’s electrical signals. It enables physicians to assess heart rhythm and overall cardiac function, offering valuable insights into heart health.

    With HealWell, you can skip the trip to the hospital. Our trained professionals collect samples at your home, which are then sent to labs. Test results are shared digitally, ensuring a seamless, accurate, and convenient experience.

  • Purpose of an ECG Test

    The ECG test is a critical diagnostic tool used to identify irregularities in the heart's rhythm, electrical activity, or structure. It can help detect:

    • Irregular heartbeats (arrhythmias)

    • Damage resulting from a heart attack

    • Enlargement of heart chambers or other structural abnormalities

  • How ECG Aids in Diagnosing Heart Conditions

    By examining the heart’s electrical signals, an ECG test can reveal:

    • Coronary artery blockages

    • Delays or disruptions in electrical conduction

    • Signs of previous cardiac episodes

    Early detection through ECG plays a vital role in the effective management and treatment of heart diseases.

  • When Should You Consider an ECG Test at Home?

    Knowing when to undergo an ECG is essential for timely diagnosis. The test is recommended in the following scenarios:

  • Individuals with Cardiac Symptoms

    People experiencing chest pain, palpitations, breathlessness, or dizziness should consider an ECG to rule out or confirm underlying heart conditions.

  • Patients with Chronic Heart Conditions

    Those diagnosed with long-term conditions such as arrhythmias, high blood pressure, or coronary artery disease benefit from regular ECG monitoring at home.

  • Elderly Individuals Requiring Routine Monitoring

    At-home ECG testing provides a comfortable and accessible option for seniors who need consistent cardiac health checks.

  • Post-Operative or Post-Cardiac Event Monitoring

    Patients recovering from heart surgery or a heart attack can use home ECG services for ongoing monitoring, reducing the need for frequent hospital visits.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it advisable to move during an ECG test?

To ensure accurate results, it is recommended to stay as still as possible during the ECG, as any movement may cause interference or distort the readings.

Which heart conditions can an ECG detect?

An ECG can aid in identifying various cardiac conditions, including irregular heart rhythms (arrhythmias), myocardial infarction (heart attack), cardiomyopathy, and complications arising from electrolyte imbalances.

Is fasting necessary before undergoing an ECG?

Fasting is not required prior to an ECG; however, it is advisable to avoid caffeine and heavy meals beforehand to help ensure the most accurate results.

What is the duration of an ECG test?

An ECG usually takes about 10 to 15 minutes to complete, with the results promptly transmitted to your healthcare provider for review
